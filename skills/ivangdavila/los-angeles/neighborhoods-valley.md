# Valley Neighborhoods — San Fernando Valley

The Valley gets a bad rap but offers excellent value: more space, safer neighborhoods, entertainment industry proximity. Trade-off: 10-15°F hotter in summer.

---

## Valley Overview

### Why Consider the Valley

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| 20-30% cheaper rent | 10-15°F hotter |
| More space (houses, yards) | "Over the hill" stigma |
| Very safe neighborhoods | Requires car |
| Studios nearby (Warner, Disney, Universal) | Less walkable |
| Family-friendly | Less trendy |

### Temperature Reality

| Season | Coast | Valley |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Summer high | 78°F | 95°F |
| Heat wave | 85°F | 105-115°F |
| Winter | Similar | Similar |

**AC is mandatory in the Valley.** Budget extra for electricity.

## Studio City

### Overview
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Vibe** | Entertainment industry, quiet, family |
| **1BR Rent** | $2,000-2,800 |
| **Safety** | ★★★★☆ Safe |
| **Transit** | B Line terminus (North Hollywood) |
| **Parking** | Good |

### Who It's For
- Entertainment industry workers
- Families
- People wanting Valley + some walkability
- Universal Studios commuters

### Pros
- Ventura Blvd restaurants/shops
- Near Universal, CBS
- Relatively walkable (for Valley)
- Good schools
- Safe

### Cons
- Hot summers
- Over the hill from Westside
- Traffic on Ventura
- Getting expensive

### Ventura Boulevard
Main strip with restaurants, cafes, shopping. Most walkable part of Valley.
