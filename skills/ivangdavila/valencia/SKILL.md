## Valencia-Specific Traps

- **Siesta hours** ‚Äî Many shops close 14:00-17:00. Supermarkets stay open.
- **Spanish bureaucracy** ‚Äî NIE, empadronamiento, gestor√≠a often needed. Budget time and patience.
- **Rental scams** ‚Äî Never pay without visiting. Idealista is safest. Watch for fake listings.
- **August shutdown** ‚Äî Many businesses close 2-3 weeks. Plan accordingly.
- **Paella timing** ‚Äî Valencians eat paella at LUNCH (13:00-15:00), never dinner. Tourist restaurants ignore this.
- **Beach vs Centro trade-off** ‚Äî Beach areas are 20-30 min from Centro. Choose based on lifestyle.
- **Valenciano pressure** ‚Äî Some government services prefer Valenciano. Spanish always accepted legally.
- **Fallas chaos** ‚Äî March 15-19: amazing but LOUD. Book early or leave entirely.
- **Northern European expectations** ‚Äî Things move slower. Adapt to local pace.
- **Banking hours** ‚Äî Most banks close at 14:00. Digital banking is easier.

## Legal Awareness (Non-EU)

Key requirements for non-EU citizens:
- **Digital Nomad Visa**: Available since 2023, requires ‚Ç¨2,520/month income
- **Aut√≥nomo**: Self-employed visa requires business plan and startup capital
- **Non-lucrative Visa**: For retirees/wealthy, no work permitted

## EU Citizen Advantage

EU citizens can:
- Move without visa, work immediately
- Register (empadronamiento) and get NIE within weeks
- Access public healthcare with EHIC initially, full access after registration
- Bring non-EU family members relatively easily
