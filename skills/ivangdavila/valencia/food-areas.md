## Ruzafa — The Foodie Hub

### Why Here
Valencia's undisputed food neighborhood. Highest concentration of quality restaurants, best brunch scene, most international options.

### Best Streets
- **Calle Literato Azorín**: Restaurant row
- **Calle Sueca**: Mix of bars and restaurants
- **Calle Cadiz**: More dining options
- **Around Mercado de Ruzafa**: Local spots

### What to Eat
- Brunch (weekends)
- Modern Spanish
- Asian cuisine
- Cocktails
- Specialty coffee

### Top Picks
| Restaurant | Type | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| Federal | Brunch | €15-25 |
| Canalla Bistro | Modern Spanish | €30-45 |
| Ma Khin | Thai | €20-30 |
| La Salvaora | Contemporary Valencian | €25-40 |
| Bluebell Coffee | Coffee | €5-10 |

### When to Go
- **Brunch**: 10:00-13:00 weekends (book ahead)
- **Dinner**: 21:00-23:00 (book Friday/Saturday)
- **Drinks**: 19:00+ terrazas fill up

### Tips
- Walk around before deciding — many options
- Quality is generally high, hard to go wrong
- More expensive than traditional areas but worth it
- Vegetarian/vegan friendly

## El Carmen — Tapas & Night Bites

### Why Here
Historic atmosphere, best nightlife area, good for bar hopping with tapas. More touristy but still enjoyable.

### Best Streets
- **Calle Caballeros**: Main bar strip
- **Plaza del Negrito**: Terrazas
- **Near Torres de Serranos**: Quieter spots
- **Plaza del Carmen**: Mix of options

### What to Eat
- Traditional tapas
- Late-night bites
- Wine and vermouth
- Casual meals between sights

### Top Picks
| Restaurant | Type | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| Bar Pilar | Clóchinas (mussels) | €10-15 |
| Café de las Horas | Drinks, light food | €10-20 |
| Sant Jaume | Traditional | €15-25 |
| Tasca del Angel | Tapas | €12-20 |

### When to Go
- **Pre-lunch vermouth**: 12:00-14:00
- **Evening tapas**: 20:00-22:00
- **Night bites**: 00:00-03:00

### Tips
- Tourist trap risk is higher here — check Google reviews
- Avoid restaurants with picture menus outside
- Side streets often better than main plazas
- Good for pre-/post-sightseeing bites

## Centro (La Seu / Mercado) — Traditional & Tourist

### Why Here
Near major attractions, traditional Spanish restaurants, convenient for sightseeing days.

### Best Areas
- **Around Mercado Central**: Market eating
- **Plaza de la Reina**: Tourist central (mixed quality)
- **Calle de la Paz**: More upscale
- **Near Lonja**: Some gems

### What to Eat
- Traditional Valencian
- Market tapas
- Rice dishes
- Formal Spanish dining

### Top Picks
| Restaurant | Type | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| Mercado Central stalls | Tapas | €10-20 |
| Casa Roberto | Paella | €20-30 |
| La Riuà | Rice dishes | €18-28 |
| El Poblet | Fine dining | €100-150 |

### When to Go
- **Market**: 08:00-14:00 (closes 14:30)
- **Lunch**: 13:30-15:30
- **Dinner**: 21:00-23:00

### Tips
- Plaza de la Reina has tourist traps — be selective
- Mercado Central is legit, eat there confidently
- Book rice restaurants in advance
- Some best restaurants are hidden on side streets

## El Cabanyal — Seafood & Authenticity

### Why Here
Historic fishing neighborhood with the most authentic seafood and rice restaurants. Less pretentious, more local.

### Best Streets
- **Calle de la Reina**: Main food street
- **Near Mercado del Cabanyal**: Local joints
- **Paseo Marítimo edge**: Some good spots

### What to Eat
- Seafood (obviously)
- Traditional rice dishes
- Old-school tapas
- Fresh fish

### Top Picks
| Restaurant | Type | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| Casa Montaña | Historic bodega | €20-35 |
| Casa Carmela | Paella | €20-30 |
| La Pascuala | Traditional | €15-25 |
| Mercado del Cabanyal | Market | €5-15 |

### When to Go
- **Lunch**: Best time for rice/seafood (13:30-15:30)
- **Evening tapas**: 20:00-22:00

### Tips
- Worth the trip from Centro (20-30 min)
- Casa Montaña alone justifies the visit
- More local than touristy
- Combine with beach visit

## Malvarrosa — Beach Dining

### Why Here
Beachfront location, paella with sea views, chiringuito culture.

### What's Available
- Chiringuitos (beach bars)
- Rice restaurants along promenade
- Casual beach fare

### Top Picks
| Restaurant | Type | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| La Pepica | Historic paella | €25-35 |
| Levante | Traditional rice | €20-30 |
| La Marcelina | Seafood | €25-35 |
| Various chiringuitos | Casual | €15-25 |

### When to Go
- **Lunch**: 13:30-16:00 (paella time)
- **Sunset drinks**: 19:00-21:00 (chiringuitos)

### Tips
- Touristy but quality is usually good
- Book paella 30-45 min ahead
- Sunset views worth premium
- Summer = crowded, book ahead

## La Albufera — Paella Pilgrimage

### Why Here
THE place for authentic paella. Rice paddies, lagoon, the source.

### How to Get There
- **By car**: 20 min from Centro
- **By bus**: Line 25 (45 min)
- **By bike**: Possible, 40 min

### What to Eat
- Paella Valenciana (the original)
- All i pebre (eel stew)
- Arroz dishes

### Top Picks (El Palmar village)
| Restaurant | Price | Notes |
|------------|-------|-------|
| Nou Racó | €25-35 | Rice field views |
| Bon Aire | €20-30 | Family-run classic |
| Mateu | €20-30 | Traditional |

### When to Go
- **Lunch only**: 13:00-16:00
- **Combine with**: Boat ride (sunset ideal)
- **Best days**: Weekdays less crowded

### Tips
- This is THE paella experience
- Book ahead on weekends
- Plan 3-4 hours (travel + meal + boat)
- Order 30-45 min before eating
